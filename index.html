About Our Craft

Welcome to Petzval Pictures, where we practice the elegant art of wet plate collodion ambrotype photography in the heart of Ahwatukee, Arizona. Using techniques that date back to the 1850s, we create stunning, one-of-a-kind portraits that capture the soul and essence of our subjects in ways that modern photography simply cannot replicate.


Each ambrotype is a unique piece of art, handcrafted using historical processes that involve coating glass plates with light-sensitive chemicals, exposing them in an antique camera, and developing them on the spot. The result is a hauntingly beautiful image with unparalleled depth and character that will be treasured for generations.


Our studio specializes in portrait photography that transcends time, creating heirloom-quality images with the aesthetic beauty of the Victorian era combined with the artistic vision of today.

The Wet Plate Process

1

Preparation

We hand-cut and clean glass plates, then coat them with collodion, a syrupy substance that creates a light-sensitive surface when treated with silver nitrate.

2

Exposure

The plate must be exposed while still wet. Your portrait is captured using our vintage large-format camera and Petzval lens, requiring stillness and patience.

3

Development

Immediately after exposure, we rush the plate to our darkroom where it's developed, fixed, and varnished—all within minutes of your sitting.

4

Completion

The finished ambrotype is a positive image on glass—a unique, unrepeatable work of art that captures a moment in time with ethereal beauty.
